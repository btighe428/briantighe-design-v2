# Example LinkedIn Artifact — Known Good

This file contains a reference LinkedIn post that exemplifies the voice, structure, and register the distribution skill should produce. Study this example before generating new artifacts. The artifact below corresponds to a hypothetical essay on "Experiment Interfaces."

---

## Artifact

Product teams in 2026 are discovering an uncomfortable truth: the interfaces they design for end users are often worse instruments for experimentation than the internal tools their data teams built five years ago.

The pattern is specifically visible in consumer DTC and B2B SaaS categories, where the gap between "interface users see" and "interface designed for measurable iteration" has widened meaningfully since AI-assisted prototyping tools arrived.

This is Experiment Interfaces — the design discipline of building user-facing UI with testability velocity as a primary design constraint, not a downstream engineering consideration.

The mechanism is subtle but consequential.

Traditional UI design prioritizes aesthetic coherence, user-flow clarity, and brand expression — experimentation capability is assumed to emerge from proper instrumentation downstream.

Experiment Interfaces inverts this: the capacity to iterate the UI in days rather than weeks is treated as a first-class design requirement, on par with visual hierarchy.

At HelloFresh, treating the onboarding flow as an Experiment Interface produced 23 measurable iterations in 90 days — an iteration velocity that directly enabled the +$75M conversion program we ran that year.

For product leaders reading this: look at your highest-traffic user flow and ask whether it was designed as an Experiment Interface or as a conventional UI.

If the latter, your next quarter of growth work is constrained by a design decision made months ago.

## What this artifact demonstrates

**Opening**: A specific claim about an observable pattern in 2026, grounded in a named category. Not a question, not a hot take.

**Precedent**: References "consumer DTC and B2B SaaS categories" as empirical anchor without inventing a statistic.

**Framework introduction**: Names "Experiment Interfaces" explicitly, with the framework definition in the same sentence.

**Mechanism**: Two sentences that contrast traditional UI design with Experiment Interfaces. The contrast structure is load-bearing — it makes the framework legible by distinction.

**Receipt**: Specific, quantified HelloFresh example. Uses Brian's real receipt (+$75M) rather than inventing numbers.

**Implication**: Specific to the reader's decision-making. Names a concrete action ("look at your highest-traffic user flow") rather than generic exhortation.

**Link**: On its own line, preceded by a blank line, no additional CTA.

**Character count**: Approximately 1,580 characters. Well within the 1,400–1,700 target.

## What would have made this artifact worse

- Opening with a question ("Have you ever noticed that...?") — violates voice markers
- Including hashtags (#productdesign #experimentation) — violates voice markers
- Adding emoji as visual accent — violates voice markers
- Using "10x" or "game-changing" to describe the iteration velocity — violates voice markers
- Inviting engagement ("What's your take? Comment below.") — violates engagement-fishing prohibition
- Self-referencing ("As a Principal Designer at Yahoo Mail...") — violates credential-signaling prohibition
