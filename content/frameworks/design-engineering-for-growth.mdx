## Definition

**Design engineering for growth** is a discipline that sits between product design, front-end engineering, and growth. Its core premise: the shippable artifact — not the deck, the spec, or the wireframe — is the unit of account. The designer who can ship is worth an order of magnitude more than one who can only specify.

## Why it matters

Growth teams historically optimized funnels built by other people. Design teams historically handed off specs to engineers. Front-end engineers historically implemented what they were told. The three disciplines were siloed because the tools made them siloed — you could not be excellent at all three in a single day's work.

After 2024, the tools collapsed the silos. Design engineering for growth is the discipline that makes the collapse productive.

## The practice

A design engineer for growth:

- Ships the artifact that tests the thesis, not a spec for someone else to ship.
- Treats the running system as the source of truth about design intent.
- Owns measurement — the prototype ships with its own instrumentation.
- Writes and publishes the reasoning behind each artifact as part of the artifact's compounding record.
