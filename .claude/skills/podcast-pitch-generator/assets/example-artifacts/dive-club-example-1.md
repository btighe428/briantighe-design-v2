Ridd —

Your episode with the Linear design lead on "taste as infrastructure" made the point that design decisions propagate forward only when the decision itself is legible to engineers. The essay I just published works the same axis from the positioning side.

The framework is Prototype-Led Positioning: treating a working, shippable prototype as the primary positioning artifact, with the deck (if it exists) as a derivative summary. The argument is that the 2024–2026 AI-coding generation has inverted the economics — a working prototype is now faster to produce than a positioning deck, more legible to the team that has to build against it, and produces better market feedback in 72 hours than six months of messaging testing. At Yahoo Mail I used this pattern on one OrbitMail feature to collapse a four-month stalled positioning decision into one week of Claude Code sessions.

Three angles I could bring on the show:

- **Craft mechanics:** What a positioning prototype actually looks like — the specific files, the typography decisions, the interaction primitives — versus what a deck slide looks like when the same argument is forced into that medium.
- **Industry frame:** How this pattern rewrites the relationship between product design and product marketing, and why the design-engineering seat is the one that absorbs the role.
- **Contrarian frame:** Why Figma mockups are worse for positioning than working prototypes despite feeling closer — the mockup-as-deck critique.

Twenty years shipping interfaces; currently Principal Product Designer at Yahoo Mail; writing a research program on design engineering for growth at briantighe.design.

If any of these angles could fill a useful hour for your audience, I would be grateful for a 15-minute call to refine the topic before committing to a recording.
