## Definition

**The working prototype as primary artifact** is the principle that a running, shippable prototype — not a deck, a spec, or a wireframe — is the object of critique, alignment, and compounding in a product practice.

## Why it matters

The 1847 patent office did not grant patents on sketches of machines. It granted them on working models — physical artifacts that demonstrated the mechanism. The discipline of evaluating ideas against the physics of their implementation is not new. What is new is that, in software, the implementation cost has fallen below the specification cost.

When a working prototype is cheaper than its spec, the spec is a regression. The artifact becomes the source of truth and the spec becomes a derivative summary — a map for people who cannot run the territory.

## Three properties

The working prototype, unlike the deck:

1. **Tests the thesis against the medium.** A positioning statement that cannot survive contact with an implemented interface is a wish, not a statement.
2. **Legibly compounds.** A prototype is a git repo. It forks, extends, and reuses. A deck is a file.
3. **Produces feedback in hours, not months.** You can show it to a stranger. You can publish a URL.
