# Aggregator Profiles — curator editorial angles and submission mechanics

Each entry includes: audience, editorial angle, submission mechanics, pitch-worthy essay types, and a stale-as-of date. Refresh entries with a stale date older than 6 months before pitching.

## Lenny's Newsletter (guest posts)
- **Curator:** Lenny Rachitsky
- **Audience:** ~1M+ product/growth operators
- **Editorial angle:** Tactical product and growth playbooks with concrete metrics. Strong preference for operator-authored pieces backed by a receipt.
- **Submission:** Direct pitch to lenny@lennysnewsletter.com OR DM on X. He publishes guest posts when the framework is novel and the receipt is legible.
- **Pitch-worthy when:** Essay has a named framework + a specific operator metric (conversion lift, retention delta, revenue impact).
- **Not a fit:** Pure opinion, speculative trends, visual design critique.
- **Stale as of:** 2026-04-19

## Not Boring (Packy McCormick)
- **Curator:** Packy McCormick
- **Audience:** ~250K operators/investors
- **Editorial angle:** Thematic essays tying industry dynamics to specific companies; Packy rarely links out but cites work he considers thesis-shaping.
- **Submission:** Direct reply on Twitter/X; Packy does not accept cold email for feature consideration. Citation comes from his own discovery, not inbound pitches.
- **Pitch-worthy when:** Essay contains a thesis that upgrades Packy's existing mental model on a category he's already writing about (AI, fintech, infrastructure). Frame the DM as "this changed how I'd think about X" not "please feature."
- **Not a fit:** Tactical how-to, product design craft.
- **Stale as of:** 2026-04-19

## Sidebar.io
- **Curator:** Sacha Greif
- **Audience:** ~100K designers
- **Editorial angle:** Five curated links per day for designers. High-quality writing about design, process, research; also tools and case studies.
- **Submission:** sidebar.io/submit form, or DM Sacha on X with context.
- **Pitch-worthy when:** Essay is about design practice, design engineering, or a named framework that helps designers ship.
- **Not a fit:** Growth without a design craft element, pure product management content.
- **Stale as of:** 2026-04-19

## Dense Discovery (Kai Brach)
- **Curator:** Kai Brach
- **Audience:** ~50K designers + thoughtful operators
- **Editorial angle:** Careful, essayistic links. Kai favors longform over tactical. He skips anything that reads as growth-hacker content; he elevates reflective operator writing and category-defining work.
- **Submission:** hello@densediscovery.com with a short note explaining the essay's idea. No pitch-heavy language.
- **Pitch-worthy when:** Essay is essayistic, operator-voiced, and category-defining. Prototype-led positioning essay is a fit.
- **Not a fit:** How-to, listicles, "10 ways to...".
- **Stale as of:** 2026-04-19

## UX Collective (Medium)
- **Curator:** Fabricio Teixeira, Caio Braga
- **Audience:** ~700K+ via Medium publication
- **Editorial angle:** Design practice, research, critique, systems thinking. They republish from personal blogs with canonical link back.
- **Submission:** uxdesign.cc/submission-guidelines — request editor review via Medium's publication flow. They prefer original or canonical-republished.
- **Pitch-worthy when:** Essay is about design practice with a craft element. Essay on prototype-led positioning fits; essay on AI infrastructure decisions does not.
- **Note:** Republishing on Medium with canonical pointing to briantighe.design is the correct pattern — do NOT remove the essay from briantighe.design or republish without canonical tag.
- **Stale as of:** 2026-04-19

## Smashing Magazine
- **Curator:** Vitaly Friedman (Editor-in-Chief), plus section editors
- **Audience:** ~200K frontend and design practitioners
- **Editorial angle:** Technical and design craft; in-depth articles with specific techniques. Shorter pieces get curated into their weekly roundup.
- **Submission:** editors@smashingmagazine.com for feature articles; newsletter editor for roundup inclusion.
- **Pitch-worthy when:** Essay has a technical craft component (typography system, Tufte layout, MDX pipeline) or a growth-engineering framework.
- **Stale as of:** 2026-04-19

## A List Apart
- **Curator:** Editorial board
- **Audience:** ~80K web practitioners
- **Editorial angle:** Durable, thoughtful writing on web practice. Lower cadence, higher curation bar.
- **Submission:** alistapart.com/contribute — they ask for a pitch-first before the full draft.
- **Pitch-worthy when:** Essay is durable, not news-cycle-dependent, and speaks to web practitioners specifically.
- **Stale as of:** 2026-04-19

- **Editorial angle:** Technical operator writing with specifics. Gergely cites work that upgrades his readers' mental model on engineering practice.
- **Submission:** Gergely rarely accepts cold submissions. Citation via X/LinkedIn engagement is more effective than email pitch.
- **Pitch-worthy when:** Essay is at the intersection of engineering and operator practice; design-engineering content has moderate fit.

## Stratechery (Ben Thompson)
- **Curator:** Ben Thompson
- **Audience:** ~35K paid subscribers
- **Editorial angle:** Strategic analysis of tech industry dynamics. Ben cites work that sharpens his thesis on aggregation theory, platform strategy, or industry structure.
- **Submission:** No submission form. Citation is earned via the work's fit to his ongoing analytical arc.
- **Pitch-worthy when:** Essay contains strategic argument that intersects with platform dynamics or category formation. Frame engagement as genuine intellectual contribution, not pitch.
- **Stale as of:** 2026-04-19

## Benedict Evans weekly
- **Curator:** Benedict Evans
- **Audience:** ~185K
- **Editorial angle:** Curated weekly roundup of tech-industry reading. Citation is merit-based; he reads widely and links what he finds analytically interesting.
- **Submission:** No submission mechanism; citation is discovery-based. Engagement on X with substantive comments over time increases discovery probability.

## Default posture for unprofiled aggregators

2. Submission mechanics (email, form, DM)
3. One recent feature that matches the pitched essay's angle
4. Warm or cold posture
