## Definition

**Prototype-led positioning** (PLP) is the practice of treating a working, shippable prototype as the primary positioning artifact of a product, brand, or category. The deck — if produced at all — becomes a derivative summary written from the prototype, not toward it.

## Why it matters

For twenty years before 2024, positioning exercises produced the same artifact: a PowerPoint deck. The deck was the deliverable because the cost of producing a working prototype exceeded the cost of producing a deck by roughly one hundred to one. After the 2024–2026 generation of AI-assisted prototyping tools, that ratio inverted. A working prototype is now faster to produce than a positioning deck, more legible to the team that has to build against it, and produces better market feedback than any amount of messaging testing.

## The three disciplines

1. Every positioning claim must ship as a working artifact before it is allowed into a deck.
2. The artifact, not the deck, is the object of critique in the positioning review.
3. The positioning deck, if produced at all, is written *from* the prototype — a derivative summary for people who cannot use the prototype themselves.
