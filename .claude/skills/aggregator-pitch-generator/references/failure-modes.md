# Failure Modes — Aggregator Pitch Generator

Halt generation and surface the concern to Brian rather than proceed silently on any of the following.

## 1. Profile stale
**Signal:** Target aggregator profile in `aggregator-profiles.md` has stale-as-of date > 6 months old.
**Handling:** Stop. Tell Brian which profile needs refreshing. Offer to draft a refresh pass (check curator name, recent features, current submission mechanics) before generating the pitch.

## 2. Essay-aggregator mismatch
**Signal:** The essay's primary framework does not plausibly fit the aggregator's editorial angle.
**Example:** An essay on typography choices for e-ink displays is not a fit for Lenny's Newsletter (operator/growth); it is a fit for Smashing Magazine or Sidebar.
**Handling:** Stop. Recommend a different target or say "this essay does not have a natural aggregator fit; consider holding for a future essay or picking one aggregator where the miss is mild."

## 3. Curator change
**Signal:** Public signal (bio update, recent post, departure announcement) suggests the curator has changed or the publication has paused since the profile was last refreshed.
**Handling:** Stop. Flag for manual verification before sending.

## 4. Same-week collision
**Signal:** Brian has already pitched the target within the last 14 days with a different essay, or the target has featured Brian's work within the last 30 days.
**Handling:** Warn. Outreach fatigue degrades relationships. Recommend a 30+ day cooling period unless the curator explicitly opened a door to follow-up.

## 5. Voice performance
**Signal:** Draft reads as performative warmth — flattery without specificity, "love your newsletter," generic "big fan."
**Handling:** Rewrite. Every personal opener must reference a specific recent piece the curator wrote, with a specific observation about it. If the generator cannot produce this from the profile or from web-fetched curator content, stop and ask Brian which recent piece to reference.

## 6. No receipt where one is required
**Signal:** Target aggregator (Lenny, Pragmatic Engineer, Not Boring) requires operator specificity, but the essay lacks a concrete metric or case study.
**Handling:** Stop. Do not pitch these curators with speculative framework essays. Offer to pitch softer-specificity targets (Dense Discovery, UX Collective, Kottke) instead.

## 7. Fabricated specifics
**Signal:** Draft contains any metric, company name, case study, or timeline that does not appear in the source essay.
**Handling:** Hard stop. Never fabricate. Rewrite using only essay-derived specifics or remove the claim.

## 8. Framework name mismatch
**Signal:** The framework name cited in the pitch does not match the exact capitalized form in the essay.
**Handling:** Rewrite. Use the exact capitalized form ("Prototype-Led Positioning," not "prototype-led positioning" or "PLP").

## 9. Category overreach
**Signal:** Pitch uses phrases like "thought leadership," "personal brand," "content strategy," or positions Brian as a "creator" or "influencer."
**Handling:** Rewrite. These phrases violate voice markers and signal the wrong operating lineage.

## 10. Multiple frameworks in one pitch
**Signal:** Draft references more than one of Brian's named frameworks in a single pitch.
**Handling:** Rewrite to feature only the essay's primary framework. Framework proliferation dilutes each.

## When in doubt

Surface the concern to Brian rather than ship a degraded draft. The cost of a paused generation is minutes. The cost of a poorly-calibrated pitch to a curator Brian cares about is relationship-scale.
