# Weekly Distribution Workflow

This document specifies the exact operational cadence that stitches the distribution skill into your publishing workflow. Read once; refer back weekly during the first six weeks of operation until the rhythm becomes automatic.

## Part I: The Anchoring Principle

The cadence below is anchored to one immovable commitment: every Monday morning, you verify that the prior week's distribution work has completed, and you schedule the current week's distribution work. This is a 15-minute weekly ritual that does not move regardless of travel, holidays, or other disruptions. Missing this ritual more than twice per quarter indicates the system has failed and requires restructuring, not continued operation at lower fidelity.

Historical precedent worth noting: Ben Thompson has published Stratechery's daily update for more than a decade without missing a single weekday except scheduled vacation. The cadence itself is the moat. The cadence is what enables compound authority accumulation because it produces predictable reader expectation, which in turn produces the habitual return-visit behavior that converts casual readers into subscribers.

Your version of this cadence is lighter than Thompson's because your conservative publishing rhythm is two essays per month rather than five per week. But the anchoring principle is identical: the ritual is non-negotiable even when the individual execution is imperfect.

## Part II: The Four-Week Rolling Cadence

Distribution work follows a four-week rolling pattern tied to your two-essays-per-month publishing cadence. The pattern assumes essays publish on alternating Wednesdays, with distribution work filling the surrounding days.

### Week A (Essay publication week)

**Monday morning (15 min):** Weekly distribution ritual. Verify prior week's Typefully schedules sent correctly. Check Plausible dashboard for prior week's performance. Review Linear for any editorial flags surfaced by the distribution skill's recent generations.

**Tuesday morning (30 min):** Final essay editorial pass. The essay that publishes Wednesday should be in its final state by Tuesday evening, with no open revisions. This creates the clean publication anchor that distribution work depends on.

**Wednesday morning (10 min):** Publish the essay by merging to main. Ring 1 mechanical distribution runs automatically. Confirm via Discord/Slack webhook that the post-publish workflow completed successfully.

**Wednesday afternoon (45 min):** Invoke the distribution skill and generate LinkedIn and X drafts. Perform the 15–25 minute editorial review pass. Schedule via Typefully — LinkedIn for Monday 8:30 AM Eastern of Week B, X thread for Wednesday 11:00 AM Eastern of Week B.

**Thursday (no distribution work):** Reserved for content production on next essay or Ring 3 relational work.

**Friday afternoon (30 min):** Ring 3 relational work. Outreach to one newsletter operator or podcast host, personalized around the essay published Wednesday. This is the highest-ROI distribution work of the week and should not be skipped.

### Week B (Post-publication distribution week)

**Monday 8:30 AM:** Typefully publishes the LinkedIn post. Monitor engagement for the first 90 minutes and respond to any substantive comments during your morning email block.

**Monday evening (15 min):** Weekly distribution ritual for the current week. Verify LinkedIn post went live correctly. Review initial engagement signals.

**Wednesday 11:00 AM:** Typefully publishes the X thread. Monitor first-hour engagement.

**Wednesday afternoon (30 min):** If the X thread has gained traction (20+ reposts or 5,000+ impressions in the first three hours), prepare a follow-up response to any substantive replies. If it has not, move on — X engagement in 2026 is high-variance and over-investing in response to a thread that did not land produces no return.

**Thursday (no distribution work):** Reserved for next essay production or Ring 3 work.

**Friday afternoon (30 min):** Ring 3 relational work. Different operator from last Friday's outreach; rotate through your cultivated list.

### Week C (Next essay production week)

No distribution work this week. Focus is entirely on producing the next essay. The distribution skill will run again next Wednesday of Week A.

The discipline in Week C is *not* adding distribution work to fill the calendar. The four-week cadence works because it alternates deep production work with distribution work rather than attempting to maintain both simultaneously. Operators who attempt simultaneous production and distribution produce worse essays and worse distribution than operators who alternate.

### Week D (Pre-publication preparation week)

**Monday morning (15 min):** Weekly distribution ritual.

**Tuesday evening (45 min):** Claude Research backbone report generation for the essay that will publish the following Wednesday. The research generation runs while you do other things; the active work is the subsequent editorial verification.

**Wednesday–Friday:** Editorial verification, framework origination, and voice synthesis work on the essay. This is the 15–20 hours of focused editorial work distributed across three evenings.

**Friday afternoon (30 min):** Ring 3 relational work.

**Weekend:** Final polish and typographic refinement on the essay. By Sunday evening, the essay should be ready for final editorial pass on Monday of Week A.

## Part III: Time Budget Verification

Summing the weekly time allocations across the four-week cycle:

| Week | Distribution Skill Work | Ring 3 Work | Ritual | Subtotal |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | 1h 25min | 30min | 15min | 2h 10min |
| B | 1h 15min | 30min | 15min | 2h |
| C | 0 | 30min | 15min | 45min |
| D | 0 | 30min | 15min | 45min |

Four-week total: approximately 5h 40min of distribution-specific work, which maps to roughly 1h 25min per week average — well within your 6-hour weekly budget.

The remaining ~4.5 hours per week of budget should allocate across: essay production work (distributed across Week C and Week D, roughly 8–12 hours per essay), Ring 3 relationship depth-building (approximately 1 hour per week of relationship-specific work beyond the Friday outreach), and occasional Ring 1 maintenance (approximately 1 hour per month).

If the actual distribution work consistently exceeds this budget, one of three conditions is occurring: the skill is under-performing and requires voice-markers.md refinement; the editorial review is running too deep and can be compressed; or the scope of distribution work has expanded beyond the core four-surface strategy (Substack, LinkedIn, X, Ring 3 direct outreach) and needs to be pruned back.

## Part IV: Quality Signal Tracking

The load-bearing signals to monitor weekly, in descending order of strategic importance:

**Citation velocity** (primary leading indicator). Monthly distinct LLM responses citing briantighe.design for category-adjacent queries. Baseline measurement week-one; track weekly thereafter. Target trajectory: 0 citations Month 1, 3–5 citations Month 3, 15–25 citations Month 6, 40–80 citations Month 12. If citation velocity is not growing quarter-over-quarter by Month 6, the content is not being successfully indexed by LLM retrieval systems and Ring 1 infrastructure requires audit.

**Ring 3 relationship depth**. Count of newsletter operator relationships with at least three substantive exchanges in the trailing 90 days. Target: 2 by Month 3, 5 by Month 9, 10 by Month 18. This metric matters more than newsletter subscriber count during the apparatus's first 18 months because it predicts the compounding inflection point.

**Newsletter subscriber growth rate**. Weekly net new subscribers. Less important than citation velocity and relationship depth but useful as confirmation signal. Expected range: 5–15 net new per week in Months 1–6, 20–60 per week in Months 7–18, 60–200+ per week in Months 19+.

**Essay publication streak integrity**. Consecutive two-week intervals with essay published on time. A single missed publication does not destabilize the apparatus; two consecutive misses require root-cause analysis in the weekly ritual.

**Ring 2 distribution performance** (LinkedIn impressions, X thread engagement). Tertiary signals. Useful for refining the distribution skill but not predictive of the 48-month trajectory.

The discipline in reviewing these metrics is not to over-optimize. Weekly 15-minute review is the correct cadence. Daily metric-checking produces over-reaction to noise and wastes the strategic attention that should be allocated to content production and relationship cultivation.

## Part V: What Could Break This System

Three failure modes to monitor for. Each produces a distinct early warning signal that, if caught early, allows correction before the system degrades.

**Voice drift across the distribution skill's outputs.** Early warning signal: your editorial review time per artifact creeps from 15–20 minutes to 30–45 minutes over successive months. Correction: quarterly voice-markers.md update, plus potential refresh of the example artifacts in assets/example-artifacts/.

**Ring 3 neglect.** Early warning signal: the Friday afternoon relational work block gets skipped three consecutive weeks. Correction: restructure the week to move Ring 3 work to a protected slot earlier in the week rather than relying on Friday-afternoon willpower.

**Essay production falling behind.** Early warning signal: a missed or delayed essay publication. Correction: audit whether the delay was a one-time genuine emergency or a pattern indicating that two essays per month is above sustainable capacity. If the latter, step down to one essay per month rather than missing essays — reduced cadence is strategically superior to unreliable cadence.
