# Failure Modes — Podcast Pitch Generator

Halt and surface to Brian rather than ship a degraded pitch. Podcasts are high-leverage and saturated; one poorly-calibrated pitch burns the relationship for 12+ months.

## 1. Guest-criteria mismatch
**Signal:** The show books a seniority tier or category Brian does not yet credibly occupy (e.g., My First Million books operators with specific exit stories; How I Built This books founders with scaled companies).
**Handling:** Stop. Recommend a fit-appropriate alternative or defer the pitch until credentials align.

## 2. Episode-count saturation on the angle
**Signal:** The show recently (<90 days) hosted a guest on a substantially overlapping angle.
**Handling:** Stop, or recommend a different angle that doesn't intersect the recent episode. Surface the overlap to Brian explicitly.

## 3. Host/booker change
**Signal:** Profile is stale (>6 months) or there is public signal the host has departed / the show has paused.
**Handling:** Stop. Flag profile refresh needed before pitching.

## 4. No receipt for operator-specific shows
**Signal:** Target is Lenny / Reforge / Pragmatic Engineer / My First Million, AND the essay lacks a concrete operator-specific metric or case study.
**Handling:** Stop. Do not pitch operator-shows with speculative-framework essays. Offer craft-focused shows (Design Details, Dive Club, Devouring Details) as alternatives.

## 5. Warm-intro misidentification
**Signal:** The claimed mutual contact is not actually connected to the host, or is in a non-broker relationship (e.g., they follow each other on X but do not interact).
**Handling:** Stop. Verify before asking the contact. Burning a contact on a misfire costs more than the pitch.

## 6. Multi-framework pitch
**Signal:** Draft references more than one of Brian's named frameworks.
**Handling:** Rewrite to feature the essay's primary framework only. Framework proliferation dilutes each; also signals "I don't know what this show is about."

## 7. Fabricated specifics
**Signal:** Pitch contains show history, guest references, or Brian credentials not present in source material.
**Handling:** Hard stop. Rewrite using only verified material. For recent-episode references, use `WebFetch` or ask Brian rather than invent.

## 8. Voice performance
**Signal:** Pitch reads as hustle-culture enthusiasm, LinkedIn thought-leader pastiche, or generic fan mail.
**Handling:** Rewrite. The register should match the register of the show — Lenny's warm-operator voice is different from swyx's AI-engineer voice is different from Ridd's craft-contrarian voice.

## 9. Angle overload
**Signal:** More than three angles in the pitch, or angles are variations of the same axis rather than distinct framings.
**Handling:** Rewrite. Exactly three angles, on three different interrogation axes (mechanics / industry / craft).

## 10. Asking for the full show, not the call
**Signal:** Pitch ends with "would love to be on the show" or similar full-commitment ask.
**Handling:** Rewrite. The easy ask is a 15-minute topic-refinement call. Hosts accept calls more than full-show commitments; the call is where the show decision is actually made.

## 11. Credential over-ordering
**Signal:** Credentials section leads with "writing at briantighe.design" for a show whose audience doesn't care about Brian's essays.
**Handling:** Reorder per the template's host-specific notes. Operator-at-Yahoo-Mail leads for Lenny; writer-at-briantighe.design leads for Dense Discovery surface (but that's aggregator-pitch territory, not podcast).

## When in doubt

Stop. Surface the uncertainty. Podcasts reward precision; they punish sloppiness more than almost any other distribution surface because the host has to spend an hour with the guest and knows immediately if the pitch overstated.
