# Example — Lenny's Newsletter Pitch

*Known-good reference. Essay: Prototype-Led Positioning. Intended as a feature-consideration pitch, not a guest-post pitch.*

---

**To:** lenny@lennysnewsletter.com
**Subject:** Prototype-led positioning — replacing the deck with a working artifact

Lenny —

Your recent piece on the "Figma-ification of product strategy" argued that positioning decisions now move faster than the decks that document them. I've been working on the same inversion from the operator side and wrote an essay that names the pattern.

The framework is Prototype-Led Positioning: treating a working, shippable prototype as the primary positioning artifact, with the deck (if it exists) as a derivative summary. The essay traces why the assumptions behind the deck era — that alignment requires abstraction, that customers can evaluate positioning on slides, that decks compound — have each become false since the 2024 prototyping tooling generation. At Yahoo Mail, applying this pattern to one OrbitMail feature produced the specific receipt that anchored the essay: a positioning claim we'd sat on for four months collapsed in one week of Claude Code sessions, and the shipped prototype generated more usable market feedback in the first 72 hours than six months of messaging testing.

I'm Principal Product Designer at Yahoo Mail and write about design engineering for growth.

If it fits your editorial arc on positioning and product strategy, I would be grateful for your consideration.
