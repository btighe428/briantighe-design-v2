# Podcast Pitch Template

Host-specific tone calibration required — Lenny expects warmth-with-specificity; Gergely expects technical signal; Ridd rewards contrarian confidence; swyx rewards AI-coding fluency. Structure below is the skeleton; tone per profile.

## Email pitch structure (200–300 words)

**Subject:** Host name + angle + (optional) episode-count tag. Make it scannable in a crowded inbox.

Good: `Prototype-led positioning — a guest angle for Lenny's Podcast`
Bad: `Would love to be on your podcast!`

**Paragraph 1 — Specific engagement with the show (2 sentences).**
Reference a specific recent episode guest and something that guest said that resonated or extended. Do not "love the show." Do not claim you "never miss an episode."

**Paragraph 2 — The essay and framework (3–4 sentences).**
Name the framework. State the thesis. Include the operator receipt if one exists. Make the argument legible without requiring the host to click through. The host will not click through; they will read the pitch and decide.

**Paragraph 3 — Three angles (bullet, 1–2 sentences each).**
Propose three distinct hour-long conversations from the framework:
- Angle A: operator-mechanics framing
- Angle B: strategic-industry framing
- Angle C: craft-level framing

The host picks one. This is the single most important section of the pitch — it is the difference between "interesting" and "I can book it."

**Paragraph 4 — Credentials (1–2 sentences).**
Ordered for this host's audience. Examples:
- Lenny: "Principal Product Designer at Yahoo Mail shipping growth features; writing on design engineering for growth at briantighe.design."
- Gergely: "Principal Product Designer at Yahoo Mail working at the design-engineering boundary; writing technical frameworks at briantighe.design."
- Ridd: "Twenty years of shipping interfaces; writing a research program on design engineering for growth at briantighe.design."

**Paragraph 5 — Easy ask (1 sentence).**
"If any of these angles could fill a useful hour for your audience, I would be grateful for a 15-minute call to refine the topic before committing to a recording."

**Essay URL** on its own line.

**Sign-off:** `— Brian`

## DM structure (100–150 words)

**Opener (1 sentence):** Specific episode reference.
**Middle (2 sentences):** Framework + operator receipt + one angle.
**Close (1 sentence):** Offer the call, not the full show commitment. Include essay URL.

## Warm-intro ask structure (when mutual contact exists)

Addressed to the mutual contact, not the host. Separate file.

**Paragraph 1:** Context for the contact — what Brian is writing about and why the specific show fits.
**Paragraph 2:** The explicit ask and explicit opt-out: "If you've got a good-feeling intro path, I would be grateful for a quick forward. If not, completely understood — no need to reply."
**Attachment:** The pitch draft is quoted or linked so the contact can forward without rewriting.

## Host-specific adjustments

- **Lenny's Podcast:** Lead paragraph 2 with the receipt, not the framework. Lenny filters on operator-specificity first, framework novelty second.
- **The Pragmatic Engineer:** Use engineering vocabulary in paragraph 2 (design system as engineering system, MDX pipeline as infrastructure). Soften the design-vs-engineering boundary.
- **Design Details / Dive Club / Devouring Details:** Lead with craft. Receipt is optional; specificity of design decisions is required.
- **Latent Space:** Lead with the AI-coding tooling angle. Brian's use of Claude Code as a production tool is itself the proof — reference it explicitly.
- **Reforge:** Lead with growth-experiment ROI framing. Reforge audience wants framework-as-repeatable-process.
- **My First Million / How I Built This / The Knowledge Project:** Lower priority for first 12–18 months; these require more prior signal than briantighe.design has accumulated.

## What to omit from every pitch

- "Big fan," "longtime listener," "huge respect" — show specificity or do not show.
- "Would love to be a guest" — frame as topic offer, not self-promotion.
- Social-proof padding (follower counts, newsletter subscriber count).
- Meta-pitch commentary ("I know you're busy but...", "I don't usually pitch podcasts but...").
- Multi-framework bundling — each pitch rides one framework.
- Generic hashtags / emoji — never.
